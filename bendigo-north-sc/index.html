Energy & Carbon

Solar PV & Monitoring

Renewable Generation

The rooftop hosts a 50kW Solar Photovoltaic (PV) array consisting of 134 high-performance monocrystalline panels. This system is a critical component of our carbon reduction strategy, providing up to 70% of the building's peak daylight power load.

Live Performance Dashboard

Occupants can view live generation versus consumption data via the interactive tablet mounted in the main foyer. This data is also pushed to the building's cloud management platform for monthly reporting.

Energy Conservation Tips

  • Daylight Harvesting: Internal lighting will automatically dim when natural light levels are sufficient. Do not obstruct the sensors located near the windows.
  • Plug Loads: Ensure all non-essential equipment (coffee machines, printers) is switched off at the end of the day to minimize 'vampire' standby power draw.

Indoor Environment (Comfort)

HVAC & Thermal Comfort

System Design

This facility utilizes a high-efficiency Variable Refrigerant Flow (VRF) system. This advanced technology allows for simultaneous heating and cooling in different zones, which significantly improves user comfort while reducing energy waste by transferring heat between zones.

Temperature Set-Points

To balance occupant comfort with the building's energy efficiency targets, the system is programmed to the following seasonal set-points:

  • Summer: 24°C (+/- 1°C)
  • Winter: 21°C (+/- 1°C)

Natural Ventilation Mode

The high-level louvres in the atrium are automated based on external wind speed and internal CO2 sensors. When the 'Fresh Air' indicator is active on the wall console, we encourage users to open the manual sliding doors to enable cross-ventilation and reduce reliance on mechanical cooling.

Waste & Nature

Waste Sorting & Sustainability

Our Zero-Waste Goal

This facility operates on a 'Source Separation' model to divert at least 80% of operational waste from landfill. We do not provide individual under-desk bins; instead, please use the centralized waste hubs.

Standard Waste Streams

  • Yellow Bin: Co-mingled recycling (Clean plastics, glass, and aluminum).
  • Green Bin: Organic waste (Food scraps and BPI-certified compostable packaging).
  • Red Bin: General landfill (Only for items that cannot be recycled).

Specialty Recycling

A dedicated collection point for soft plastics (wrappers, film) and alkaline batteries is located in the staff lounge. These items are processed by specialty contractors to ensure they are properly repurposed into new materials.

Management & Operations

Building Operating Hours & Access

Facility Overview

The building is designed for high-occupancy community use and professional administration. Standard operating hours are 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM, Monday through Sunday. Access outside of these hours is restricted to authorized personnel only.

Access Control & Security

All main entry points are fitted with electronic strike locks and integrated with the building's central security BMS.

  • Staff Access: Use your assigned encrypted key fob at the proximity readers located at the North and South entries.
  • Visitor Access: During business hours, visitors should use the intercom located at the North Gate to speak with reception.

After-Hours & Emergency Procedures

If access is required outside of standard hours for maintenance or special events, please contact the Facilities Coordinator at least 48 hours in advance. In the event of an emergency, all electronic locks are designed to 'fail-safe' (open) upon activation of the fire alarm system.

Movement & Transport

EV Charging & Bike Storage

Electric Vehicle Infrastructure

Two Type 2 AC Charging Stations (7kW) are available in the lower car park for staff and visitor use. These stations are networked to prioritize charging during peak solar generation hours (10:00 AM - 2:00 PM).

End-of-Trip Facilities

We actively encourage sustainable commuting. Secure bike storage is located adjacent to the changing rooms and features:

  • Secure Racks: Capacity for 12 bikes with heavy-duty locking points.
  • Repair Station: Equipped with a universal floor pump, hex key sets, and tire levers for minor adjustments.
  • Locker Access: Daily-use lockers are available for riders to store helmets and gear.

Water & Utilities

Rainwater Harvesting System

Conservation Strategy

Potable water use is minimized through a sophisticated dual-plumbing system. A 20,000L underground concrete tank collects and stores runoff from the primary roof area.

Usage Zones

Filtered and treated rainwater is supplied to:

  1. All toilet flushing cisterns throughout the ground and first floors.
  2. All external landscape irrigation taps and the dedicated community garden supply line.

Maintenance & Safety

The system includes a UV sterilization unit and a triple-stage sediment filter to ensure the water is safe for non-potable use. If the 'Filter Service Required' alert is illuminated in the pump room, please notify the building manager immediately to avoid system bypass into the mains water supply.
